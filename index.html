United States of America

The Declaration of Independence was adopted by the Continental Congress on July 4, 1776, marking the formal separation of the Thirteen Colonies from Great Britain. The signing primarily took place on August 2, 1776, at Independence Hall in Philadelphia, with 56 delegates representing the colonies. This pivotal event declared the colonies as “free and independent States,” laying the foundation for the United States of America.
